## Part 1: Analysis & Synthesis (Steps 4-13)

### A. Kiyoshi Kurosawa: The Analog Ghost
*   **The Engine:** The Leaking Void. Ghosts do not haunt houses; they haunt the network. The internet is a space that has run out of room for the dead, so they begin to leak into the physical world (*Pulse*, *Cure*, *Retribution*).
*   **Visual/Sonic Motifs:**
    *   *The Forbidden Room:* Doors taped shut with red masking tape.
    *   *Low-Res Dread:* Figures appearing in the background of grainy webcam feeds.
    *   *The Sound of the Moden:* The screech of dial-up as a funeral dirge.
*   **Epistemology:** *Connectivity is Loneliness.* The more we connect, the more we invite the isolation of the void into our lives.

### B. Serial Experiments Lain: The Wired
*   **The Engine:** The Protocol Collapse. There is no difference between the real world and the "Wired." Identity is a data packet that can be modified, duplicated, or deleted.
*   **Visual Motifs:**
    *   *Power Lines:* The buzzing geometry of electrical grids as the world's nervous system.
    *   *Shadows with Spots:* Red/magenta-tinted shadows containing static.
    *   *The Bear Suit:* The costume as a secondary, safer identity.
*   **Epistemology:** *God is a Program.* Omnipresence is achieved through perfect synchronization with the network.

### C. Olivier Assayas (Demonlover): Corporate Occultism
*   **The Engine:** The Transactional Nightmare. Global capitalism is an occult ritual where the human soul is traded for market dominance.
*   **Visual Motifs:**
    *   *Cold Glass/Steel:* High-end corporate environments that feel sterile and lethal.
    *   *The Hentai/Extreme Interface:* Violence consumed through a screen as a form of "Safe" transgression.
*   **Epistemology:** *Evil is a Commodity.* We don't summon demons; we buy them.

### D. Clive Barker: The Puzzle Box
*   **The Engine:** The Pleasure-Pain Threshold. Transcendence through extreme physical sensation. The "Cenobites" are explorers in the further regions of experience (*Hellraiser*).
*   **Visual Motifs:**
    *   *Leather/Hooks/Chains:* The industrialization of the body.
    *   *The Labyrinth:* A geometric dimension accessed through an interface (The Box).
*   **Epistemology:** *Flesh is a Map.* The body is a puzzle that, when solved, reveals the underworld.

## Part 4: Framework Example (Step 16)

### Fragment: "The Red Tape"
*Context: A user finds a door in their house taped shut with red masking tape.*

The user peels back the tape. 
Behind the door is their own bedroom, but the resolution is 240p. 
There is a laptop on the desk. The user on the screen is looking at the door. 
The "Real" user waves. The "Screen" user screams. 
The "Real" user realizes they are the one in the forbidden room. 
The sound of dial-up internet begins to play from their own throat.

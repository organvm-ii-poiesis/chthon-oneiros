## Part 1: Analysis & Synthesis (Steps 4-13)

### A. Ari Aster: The Inevitable Inheritance
*   **The Engine:** The Family as Cult. Horror is inescapable because it is biological and historical. You are born into your role in the ritual (*Hereditary*, *Midsommar*).
*   **Visual Motifs:**
    *   *Daylight Horror:* Blinding white light that offers no protection, only exposure.
    *   *Miniatures/Dioramas:* The world as a controlled environment where the "players" have no agency.
    *   *Graphic Grief:* Physical trauma as a manifestation of emotional collapse.
*   **Narrative Structure:** The Coronation. The protagonist is systematically prepared for a horrific destiny they did not choose.
*   **Epistemology:** *Fate is a Machine.* Trauma is the fuel that powers the ancient ritual.

### B. Robert Eggers: The Authentic Haunt
*   **The Engine:** The Period Accuracy. Horror arises from the total immersion in the belief systems of the past (*The Witch*, *The Lighthouse*). If the characters believe in the devil, the devil is real.
*   **Visual Motifs:**
    *   *Natural Light:* Low-light, high-contrast images (candlelight, oil lamps).
    *   *Archaic Language:* Dialogue that acts as a summoning spell.
    *   *The Animal as Vessel:* The familiar (Black Phillip) as the gateway to the supernatural.
*   **Narrative Structure:** The Isolation. A small group is cut off from the world until their shared belief system implodes.
*   **Epistemology:** *Belief Creates Reality.* The past is a separate country with different laws of physics.

### C. Masaki Kobayashi: The Moral Ghost
*   **The Engine:** Kwaidan (Ghost Stories). Horror is the manifestation of broken honor, failed duty, and the weight of the land (*Kwaidan*, *Harakiri*).
*   **Visual Motifs:**
    *   *Painted Sets:* Highly stylized, theatrical environments that signal "Not Waking Reality."
    *   *Color Symbolism:* Specific colors (Gold, Black, Snow White) representing moral states.
    *   *The Slow Unfold:* Horror that moves with the patience of centuries.
*   **Narrative Structure:** The Requiem. A story told from the perspective of the dead or those about to die.
*   **Epistemology:** *Justice is Eternal.* The land remembers what the people forget.

### D. Ben Wheatley: The Psychedelic Earth
*   **The Engine:** The Landscape as Drug. Horror is a sensory overload triggered by the earth itself (*A Field in England*, *Kill List*).
*   **Visual Motifs:**
    *   *Strobe Editing:* Rapid-fire sequences that induce disorientation.
    *   *The Monolith:* Ancient objects or circles in the middle of mundane nature.
    *   *Sound as Weapon:* Atonal, overwhelming scores that mimic psychological collapse.
*   **Narrative Structure:** The Trip. A linear journey that becomes a circular, kaleidoscopic nightmare.
*   **Epistemology:** *The Earth is Mind-Altering.* Nature is a chaotic consciousness that we cannot navigate.

## Part 4: Framework Example (Step 16)

### Fragment: "The Dig"
*Context: A user finds a 'Corrupted' folder in their cloud storage dated 1920.*

The user finds a folder named `RECOVERY_1920`. 
Inside is a single `.txt` file written in 17th-century English.
The user reads it out loud. 
The lighting in their room shifts to a blinding, "Midsommar" white.
The sound of their keyboard typing becomes the sound of a stone chisel on rock.
The user looks at their hands. They are covered in black ink.
A notification appears: **"THE LAND HAS REMEMBERED YOUR IP ADDRESS."**

## Part 1: Analysis & Synthesis (Steps 4-13)

### A. Michael Powell: The Lethal Camera
*   **The Engine:** The Camera as Weapon. In *Peeping Tom*, the act of filming is the act of killing. The victim is forced to watch their own death through a mirror attached to the camera.
*   **Visual Motifs:**
    *   *The Viewfinder:* The frame within the frame.
    *   *The Reflection:* Mirrors capturing the moment of recognition and terror.
    *   *The Tripod:* The mechanical rigidity of the predator.
*   **Epistemology:** *To See is to Destroy.* The cinematic image is a trophy of a violation.

### B. Alfred Hitchcock: The Complicit Spectator
*   **The Engine:** The Guilt of Watching. Horror is found in the pleasure of voyeurism and the subsequent punishment for looking (*Rear Window*, *Vertigo*, *Psycho*).
*   **Visual Motifs:**
    *   *The Binoculars/Lens:* Tools that extend the gaze but isolate the gazer.
    *   *The Window:* The screen before the screen.
    *   *The Spiral:* The psychological vertigo of obsession.
*   **Epistemology:** *We are all Voyeurs.* The audience is not innocent; they are the voyeur's silent partner.

### C. Brian De Palma: The Hyper-Mediated Gaze
*   **The Engine:** The Obsession with the Image. Reality is a series of split-screens, slow-motion replays, and technical reconstructions (*Blow Out*, *Body Double*, *Dressed to Kill*).
*   **Visual Motifs:**
    *   *Split Screen:* Dual perspectives that never meet.
    *   *The Tracking Shot:* A smooth, sweeping gaze that fetishizes the environment.
    *   *The Redub:* Sound and image disconnected and then forcibly re-synced.
*   **Epistemology:** *The Truth is in the Edit.* We can only understand the world through its recorded artifacts.

## Part 4: Framework Example (Step 16)

### Episode Fragment: "The Viewfinder"
*Context: A user is cleaning an old DSLR camera.*

The user looks through the optical viewfinder. 
The lens cap is on. It should be black. 
Instead, they see their own hallway, but the perspective is from the ceiling looking down. 
They see themselves sitting on the sofa, holding the camera. 
The user on the sofa (seen through the viewfinder) looks up at the ceiling. 
The "Real" user lowers the camera. The room is normal. 
They look through the viewfinder again. 
The "Viewfinder" self is now holding a razor. 
The camera shutter clicks by itself. *CLACK.*
A print-out slides out of the camera's battery slot. It's a photo of the "Real" user's terrified face, taken from the "Viewfinder" perspective.

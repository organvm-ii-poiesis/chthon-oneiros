## Part 1: Analysis & Synthesis (Steps 4-13)

### A. Jonathan Glazer: The Inhuman Frequency
*   **The Engine:** The Alien Gaze. Displaced observation of human behavior through unblinking, often hidden cameras (*Under the Skin*, *The Zone of Interest*).
*   **Sonic Motifs:**
    *   *The Infrasound:* Sub-bass textures that produce physical anxiety without being heard as "music."
    *   *Hidden Audio:* Sounds from a "Neighboring" reality (e.g., the off-screen horrors of Auschwitz in *Zone of Interest*).
*   **Epistemology:** *Observation is Erasure.* To be seen by the alien is to be dismantled.

### B. Peter Strickland: The Acoustic Flesh
*   **The Engine:** The Fetishization of the Medium. Horror located in the act of recording, foley, and analog reproduction (*Berberian Sound Studio*, *In Fabric*).
*   **Sonic Motifs:**
    *   *Foley Violence:* The sound of vegetables being crushed representing human mutilation.
    *   *Analog Hiss:* The "Voice" of the tape.
*   **Epistemology:** *The Medium is the Victim.* The recording device records more than the event; it records the "Ghost" of the process.

### C. Hildur Guðnadóttir: The Radioactive Resonance
*   **The Engine:** The Industrial Decay. Music constructed from the environment itself—clanging pipes, humming generators, the sound of invisible toxins (*Chernobyl*, *Joker*).
*   **Sonic Motifs:**
    *   *Resonant Drones:* Textures that mimic the "Breathing" of a power plant.
    *   *Metallic Screams:* Violin techniques that sound like bowing industrial steel.
*   **Epistemology:** *The Land Screams.* Trauma is a frequency that persists in the physical structure of the earth.

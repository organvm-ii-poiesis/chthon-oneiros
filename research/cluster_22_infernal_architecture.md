## Part 1: Analysis & Synthesis (Steps 4-13)

### A. Dante Alighieri: The Moral Cartography
*   **The Engine:** The Nine Circles. The underworld is an inverted cone, a perfectly ordered system of classification where the punishment mirrors the sin (*The Divine Comedy*).
*   **Aesthetic Motifs:**
    *   *The Inscription:* "Abandon all hope, ye who enter here." (The static warning).
    *   *The Spiral:* The vertical descent through specific, themed rings.

*   **Epistemology:** *The Universe has a Grammar.* Evil is not chaotic; it is a rigid structure that can be mapped.

### B. Hieronymus Bosch: The Surreal Menagerie
*   **The Engine:** The Garden of Torment. The underworld is a landscape of biological and mechanical mutation where the everyday becomes an instrument of pain (*The Garden of Earthly Delights*).
*   **Aesthetic Motifs:**
    *   *Hybrid Bodies:* Creatures made of musical instruments, birds, and human limbs.
    *   *The Giant Ear:* Surveillance as a physical, auditory organ.
    *   *The Detail Overload:* Thousands of simultaneous atrocities in a single frame.
*   **Epistemology:** *Sin is a Mutation.* The subconscious desires manifest as physical deformations.

### C. John Milton: The Majestic Ruin
*   **The Engine:** Pandemonium. The underworld is a high-tech (for its time), grand city built in the void. It is a space of rebellion, pride, and "Darkness Visible" (*Paradise Lost*).
*   **Aesthetic Motifs:**
    *   *Architecture of Gold and Fire:* The luxury of the damned.
    *   *The Fallen Angel:* The entity that is "Noble but Broken."
    *   *The Void:* The chaos surrounding the infernal city.
*   **Epistemology:** *Hell is a State of Mind.* "The mind is its own place, and in itself / Can make a heaven of hell, a hell of heaven."
